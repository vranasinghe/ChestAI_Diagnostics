WEDAKAM Chest AI Diagnostic Guidelines

1. Pneumonia Treatment Protocol
- Clinical signs: cough, fever, sputum production, dyspnea.
- Diagnostic confirmation: Chest X-ray showing focal consolidation or patchy infiltrates.
- Treatment: Empiric antibiotic therapy. Amoxicillin 1g TID for mild/moderate community-acquired pneumonia. Levofloxacin 750mg QD for severe cases or pen-allergic patients.
- Follow-up: Repeat Chest X-ray in 6-8 weeks to confirm clearance and rule out underlying malignancy.

2. Cardiomegaly Management
- Definition: Cardiothoracic ratio > 0.50 on PA chest radiograph.
- Underlying causes: Hypertension, ischemic heart disease, cardiomyopathy, valvular heart disease.
- Workup: 12-lead ECG, Echocardiogram, thyroid panel, renal function tests.
- Treatment: ACE inhibitors, Beta-blockers, and diuretics if symptomatic heart failure is present.

3. Pneumothorax Guidelines
- Classification: Primary spontaneous (no lung disease), Secondary spontaneous (underlying COPD/emphysema), or Traumatic.
- Management of Small Spontaneous Pneumothorax (<2cm at hilum): Observation, high-flow oxygen, repeat X-ray in 4-6 hours.
- Management of Large Spontaneous Pneumothorax (>2cm at hilum) or Symptomatic: Needle aspiration (16-18G) or chest tube insertion (10-14F).

4. Atelectasis Prevention and Treatment
- Clinical features: Decreased breath sounds, tracheal deviation (if large), fever.
- Post-operative management: Incentive spirometry, chest physiotherapy, early ambulation, adequate pain control.
- Refractory cases: Bronchoscopy to clear mucus plugs.
